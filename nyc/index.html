Transportation @ Kent State – Senior Thesis

User Research, User Experience, Art Direction

Introduction & Problem

For my senior thesis project I decided to look at the public transit system that is available to Kent State University students. There has been a steady decline of use over the last 5 years. This is due to more parking spaces, and the fact that most students prefer to drive. I first set out to discover why students prefer to drive, even though there is a reliable (and free) method of transportation between campus buildings and surrounding apartments.

After interviewing students from different grade levels and backgrounds I synthesised a hypothesis. Most students are not familiar with public transit in general, or are not aware that it is available to them for free. The students who had taken the bus mentioned that they felt alienated when they first rode the bus. The surrounding area of Kent State University is somewhat rural. There are only two large metropolitan areas that support public transit (Cleveland & Akron) within a fifty mile radius of KSU. I decided to focus on getting users more familiar with the process of public transportation.

Research & Observations

Before even setting foot on a bus, I went the to web and learned about the process that new riders would go through. At first the process was confusing and left the users to figure things out on their own, thus adding to the alienation that new users feel.

This sense of alienation continued when a rider entered the actual bus. There is little assistance in the way of visuals or maps on the bus. In order for users to become more comfortable with the process, they need a crutch to lean on, something to aid them in the process.

Post-Research & Artifact

The found research identified that the end user needs a greater sense of comfort and control. If they have that, they more likely to adopt something that is outside of their comfort zone. I chose a smartphone app to be the artifact that I would explore to solve the problem.

The app below clearly lays out a linear process for the end user to follow. The user has the ability to plan movement from point A to point B. There are real-time updates that keep the user assured they won't miss a bus. Within the app there is also an on-boarding process to help educate on the different aspects of the system. All the the features of the app relate back to the idea of providing knowledge and comfort for the end user. Looking to see more? View addtional work »

Ongoing Personal Projects

Various Mediums

Watch Strap Finder App
Design Phase

This project first started as a common problem that I have when buying new watch straps. The concept of the app is that users can take a picture of their watch, crop out the current strap/band and cycle through new strap styles. The end goal would be a user purchases a strap through an affiliate that is linked to from the app. There are no other tools out there that can help you visualize what a new strap will look like with a particular watch face. This app could also create a digital collection of the various watches a user owns.

Candels & Kitsch
Brand Development Phase

Candles & Kitsch is non-digital project that I have started. The primary product is soy candles that use upcycled glassware. I love this kind of project that gets you away from the computer, and gets your hands dirty, or in this case waxy. This project is forcing me to learn more about the candle making process and how to look at objects through a different lens.

Earthquake Visualizer
Concept/Research Phase

I have always been fascinated with geology and data visualizations. The US Geological Survey has live feed of all the different quakes that are happening around the world. This data is available in JSON format, of which I know little. This project is forcing me to learn how data parsing works and how that can interface with the front-end of a site.
